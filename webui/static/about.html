Heuristic & Rule-Based Monitoring

The Transaction Monitor acts as the first line of defense. It analyzes the immediate metrics of the incoming transaction—such as absolute transaction amount, channel type (UPI, net banking, crypto), and short-term transaction velocities.

By identifying spike patterns, excessive frequencies (e.g. 5 transactions in under 2 minutes), and anomalous amounts, it quickly assigns localized risk metrics and isolates immediate structural threats.

Velocity Indicators Amount Throttling Immediate Flags

Behavioral Analysis & Profile Alignment

The Behaviour Analyser checks if the transaction matches the historic persona of the user. It evaluates physical parameters such as the device signature (`device_id`), IP Address geographical logs, and login country history.

By measuring geovelocity (the impossible geographical distance covered between subsequent transactions) and check-ins from mismatching devices, it detects account takeovers and credential stuffing attempts instantly.

Geovelocity Algorithms Device Signatures Persona Profiling

Compliance & Regulatory Guardrails

The Compliance Logger evaluates transaction risk from a regulatory standpoint. It scans user histories against standard financial safety thresholds and automatically flags suspicious patterns that warrant reporting.

It compiles comprehensive logs and ensures that any OTP triggers or direct block decisions are recorded transparently, complying with strict regulatory auditing standards.

Audit Trail logging Regulatory Thresholds Transaction Compliance

Composite Risk Scoring Engine

Once all individual agents run their tests, their scores are dynamically aggregated by a custom composite scoring engine.

Using a combination of weighted heuristics and machine learning confidence bounds, the system determines the final decision (ALLOW, OTP challenge, or BLOCK) and returns the detailed rationale.

Neo4j Graph Database Patterns

Traditional systems look at data points in isolation. We look at connections. Here are the exact relationship-based fraud clusters detected by our Neo4j layer.

Combined Fraud Network Overview

The complete Neo4j Graph schema ties accounts, physical devices, IP locations, and transactions into a single unified topology.

By mapping both direct transfers and indirect connections (like two unrelated accounts sharing a single MAC address or hardware device), the system exposes coordinated fraud operations that easily bypass simple rule engines.

Multi-hop Topology Unified Fraud Graph Network Dynamics

Coordinated Transfer Hubs

Detects high-fan-in network hubs where a single receiving account gets rapid, low-value deposits from multiple disparate accounts within a brief timeframe, followed by immediate extraction.

This pattern is typical in structured micro-laundering campaigns. Neo4j detects this instantly by querying for node degree anomalies and in-degree velocity spikes.

Degree Centrality High Fan-In Analysis Velocity Spikes

Cyclic Money Laundering (Fraud Rings)

Fraud rings transfer illicit funds through circular pathways—e.g., Account A transfers to B, B to C, and C transfers back to A. This creates the illusion of organic trade volume while obscuring the true source.

Using Neo4j's optimized Cypher queries, our database scans for cyclic paths (loops) with depth lengths of 3 to 6 hops in real-time, shutting down laundering attempts before completion.

Cyclic Queries Laundering Loops Graph Centrality

Mule Account Chains

Mule accounts act as linear relay lines where cash is quickly passed along a series of intermediary layers to obfuscate the paper trail before withdrawal.

Neo4j tracks these multi-hop linear path structures. By correlating transaction times and matching outgoing values to incoming values within close time-windows, the system exposes intermediate shell accounts.

Linear Path Analysis Time-correlation Window Multi-hop Tracking

Shared Device Clusters

A high indicator of coordinated syndicate fraud occurs when multiple distinct customer accounts login using the exact same physical hardware (`device_id`) or share a single proxy IP block.

Neo4j projects bipartite graphs linking `Account` nodes to `Device` nodes. When a device node has a high degree of unique account connections, it is flagged as an illicit fraud farm.
